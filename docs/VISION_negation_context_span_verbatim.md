## Verbatim Capture

> One important note, Negations always reference prior context, they are a wrapper for that context, and that context is often unavailable or incomplete due to semantics. That includes all surfaces where a generalized No is applied to a mess of proposals, or since the wrapper is a best guess, when it misidentifies, OR when the negation is specific with an immediate path forward, that invisibly compresses context out

> I think the concept can be expanded to this rule: "All negations represent a relationship inflection, that is avoids determinism, and is most often lossy. This is still different from negations breaking the Human intuition predicting AI statistics model."

> One tweak. It can be a relationship inflection over future context.
>
> Example
> "I would like a dog."
> "No, we should save for a house."
>
> It creates a contextual network that does not translate to statistical projects as elegantly

> So the major take away is that negations should be treated as directionally unpredictable inflections across multiple, and possibly unexpected context spans
>
> Due to how negations function in linguistics, they carry the relationship possibilities for all possible inflections
